## Introduction

Open your website on your phone right now. How long did it take to load?

If it took more than three seconds, you've already lost some of your visitors — before they read a single word. This isn't an estimate: it's the documented behaviour of people browsing the internet today. And **over 60% of web traffic already comes from mobile devices**.

A slow website isn't just a technical issue. It's a business issue.

---

## What happens when a website takes too long to load

Imagine you've invested in Google Ads, social media, or simply that a customer saw your business on Google Maps and decided to visit your website. They clicked. They waited. They waited longer. They left.

There was no second chance.

Google has been studying this behaviour for years:

> **+32%** probability of abandonment when loading time goes from 1 to 3 seconds.
> **+90%** when it exceeds 5 seconds — compared to a website that loads instantly.

Every second counts — literally.

---

## Google penalises slow websites (and rewards fast ones)

Since 2021, Google has used a set of metrics called **Core Web Vitals** to assess the technical quality of a website. In plain language: Google measures whether your site loads quickly, whether it responds well to touch on screen, and whether the content doesn't "jump around" while loading.

These factors directly influence where your website appears in search results. Two websites with similar content, in the same sector, in the same geographic area — **the faster one tends to appear higher**.

Not because Google favours technology, but because it favours user experience.

## Why so many SME websites are slow

The honest answer: because they were built with tools that prioritise ease of creation, not delivery performance.

Platforms like WordPress made it possible for anyone to create a website without knowing how to code — that was and continues to be valuable. But these platforms carry significant technical weight: unnecessary code, unoptimised images, server requests that pile up before the page appears on screen.

| Technology | Average loading time (mobile) |
|---|---|
| Typical WordPress (with themes and plugins) | 3 to 6 seconds |
| Next.js (the technology we use) | under 1.5 seconds |

The difference isn't marginal. It's the difference between a visitor who stays and one who leaves.

## How to find out if your website has this problem

Google provides a free tool called **PageSpeed Insights** — [pagespeed.web.dev](https://pagespeed.web.dev). Enter your website address and you'll get an immediate assessment, with a score from 0 to 100 for mobile devices.

| Score | Diagnosis |
|---|---|
| Below 50 | Serious problem — you're losing visitors and ranking |
| 50 to 89 | Significant room for improvement |
| 90 or above | Good — but most SME websites don't get here |
